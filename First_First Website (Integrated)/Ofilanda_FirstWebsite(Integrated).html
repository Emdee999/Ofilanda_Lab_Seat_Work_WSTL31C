Esports Prize Pools Rival Traditional Sports

Some esports tournaments, like The International for Dota 2, have prize pools exceeding $40 million, making them even larger than the payouts of many traditional sporting events. These massive rewards highlight how competitive gaming has grown into a global industry, attracting millions of viewers and professional players from around the world.

Millions Watch Live

Major esports events attract audiences of over 100 million viewers worldwide, rivaling the Super Bowl. Some of the biggest tournaments include The International (Dota 2), the League of Legends World Championship, and the CS:GO Major Championships, each drawing massive global audiences and showcasing the scale of competitive gaming.

Esports Athletes Train Like Pros

Top players often train 8–12 hours a day, focusing on strategy, reflexes, and teamwork. Their routines also include analyzing opponents' playstyles, scrimmaging with other professional teams, and even maintaining physical fitness and mental health to perform at their peak during tournaments.

GOAT Players in Esports

Dota 2: Johan "N0tail" Sundstein – 2x TI champion, over $7M in earnings.

League of Legends: Lee "Faker" Sang-hyeok – 3x World Champion, legendary mid-laner.

Counter-Strike: Oleksandr "s1mple" Kostyliev – considered one of the greatest CS:GO players ever.

Highest Earning Players & Teams

Top E-sports Player: N0tail – He has earned over $7 million throughout his career, making him not only the highest-earning Dota 2 player but also the highest-earning esports player on record.

Top E-sports Team: Team Liquid – over $56M in total winnings across various tournaments.
